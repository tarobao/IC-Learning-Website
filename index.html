- English and Math Weekend Program-

Duration:

Saturdays and Sundays, 2 hours

English:

The curriculum begins with Phonics and progresses to Advanced Grammar. Your child will improve his/her reading comprehension skills, and learn the best approach to responding to short answer questions and multiple choice questions. Your child’s lexicon will greatly expand, and he/ she will learn to associate words with mental images. This is an essential step in the literacy process.

Math:

Using the same Singaporean Math Method taught in schools, your child will learn and practice the essential basics of Addition, Subtraction, Multiplication and Division, building the confidence to approach and succeed at higher level math. Once a strong foundation has been established, your child will proceed to Algebra, Geometry, and Trigonometry.

- Read to Succeed Afterschool Program -

Duration:

Time slots vary, 1 hour

Trained instructors will assist each learner to understand story maps, and gain the ability to comprehend on a deeper level. Strategic instruction is given to assist each learner to improve his/her vocabulary and writing capabilities. Fifty minutes per class session, and students will be asked to recount the stories in their own words. Experienced instructors will assist each learner in developing his/her unique way of problem solving to produce a higher quality of academic work.

Two free classes are offered for this program. The ratio of teacher to students is 1:4, so that each learner can receive the individual attention necessary to succeed (Only iC Learning Center afterschool students are permitted to participate in this program). This program commences in September and runs throughout the entire school year. Options available for RS2 are: - twice (2x) per week or four (4x) times per week.

- Write to Succeed Afterschool Program -

Time slots vary, 1.5 hour class

Your child will be placed in a small writing group where he/she will work with an experienced instructor to greatly improve his/her writing skills. This will involve:

- Learning how to create a strong, appropriate main idea for narrative, descriptive, expository, and persuasive essays.

- Finding critical, pertinent examples and concepts to support the main idea of an essay.

- Learning how to effectively express his/her opinions, examples, research, ideas, etc. in a clear, organized manner that is intelligible to both the instructor and the reader.

- Middle School English Readiness -

Spring and Summer only

Our program is built to meet the expectations of the Common Core Standard Curriculum, and is designed to prepare students for more advanced schoolwork as well as the SHSAT. Our program is designed to help students achieve grade-level aptitude in these subjects by:

- Learning to identify and correct grammar mistakes common to students who speak English as a second language

- Developing vocabulary skills

- Applying ELA skills towards writing advanced, middle school length essays
